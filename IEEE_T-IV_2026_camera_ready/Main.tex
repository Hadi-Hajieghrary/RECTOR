\begin{abstract}
Modern multi-modal motion predictors generate diverse futures, but the executed trajectory is still often chosen by confidence rather than by explicit traffic-rule priorities. We present \textsc{RECTOR} (\textbf{R}ule-\textbf{E}nforced \textbf{C}onstrained \textbf{T}rajectory \textbf{OR}chestrator), a rule-aware selection layer that reranks candidate trajectories under the strict hierarchy Safety~$\succ$~Legal~$\succ$~Road~$\succ$~Comfort. \textsc{RECTOR} combines scene-conditioned rule activation, differentiable proxies for 24 of 28 cataloged rules, and a deterministic lexicographic selector with tier-level decision traces.

On WOMD \texttt{validation\_interactive} (12{,}800 scenarios), rule-aware reranking of a fixed candidate set lifts selected-mode compliance from 76.14\% to 84.97\% (total violations: 23.86\%\,$\to$\,15.03\%) and reduces Safety$+$Legal violations from 21.87\% to 13.15\%, with the gain concentrated in Safety-tier filtering. A weighted-sum baseline matches the same binary compliance outcomes, so the main empirical contribution is rule-aware selection over confidence-only ranking; the lexicographic selector adds a parameter-free, auditable decision rule with formal priority guarantees. Cross-evaluation on 43{,}219 augmented scenarios further shows that a conservative always-on activation policy for Safety and Legal tiers outperforms the learned applicability head in both compliance and selected-trajectory accuracy.

Verification supports the evaluator and implementation stack rather than a deployment claim: semantic invariants pass at 99.2--100\%, post-mitigation NaN/Inf propagation is 0.0\%, proxy--full-evaluator pairwise candidate-ranking concordance reaches 0.948, and mean inference latency is 7.3\,ms per sample. A 55-scenario Waymax log-replay bridge records zero overlap steps over 3{,}338.5\,s, confirming end-to-end execution but not reactive closed-loop safety. These results support explicit rule-prioritized selection as a practical and auditable way to make multi-modal prediction outputs more compliance-oriented without retraining the generator.
\end{abstract}
