\begin{abstract}
Modern multi-modal motion predictors can propose diverse futures, but the final chosen trajectory is still often selected by confidence rather than by explicit traffic-rule priorities.  We present \textsc{RECTOR} (\textbf{R}ule-\textbf{E}nforced \textbf{C}onstrained \textbf{T}rajectory \textbf{OR}chestrator), a rule-aware selection layer that preserves a generator's candidate set and reranks trajectories using the strict priority order Safety~$\succ$~Legal~$\succ$~Road~$\succ$~Comfort.  \textsc{RECTOR} combines three elements: (1)~rule-applicability estimation over a 28-rule catalog; (2)~differentiable proxy evaluators for 24 rules that assign continuous violation severities; and (3)~a deterministic lexicographic selector whose decisions are traceable at the tier level.  The current implementation includes a learned applicability head, but the broader framework also supports conservative non-learned applicability policies for audit and ablation.

On WOMD \texttt{validation\_interactive}, the canonical evaluation over 43{,}219 augmented scenarios reports strong candidate-set accuracy (minADE\,$=$\,0.687\,m, minFDE\,$=$\,1.274\,m, miss rate\,$=$\,18.46\%).  Under the proxy-24, learned-applicability setting, lexicographic selection reduces total violations from 24.06\% to 15.18\% and Safety$+$Legal violations from 22.09\% to 13.39\% relative to confidence-only selection, while also improving selected-trajectory accuracy from 2.199\,m to 2.138\,m selADE.  A weighted-sum baseline matches the same compliance rates on this benchmark and attains lower selected ADE (2.048\,m), so the contribution supported by the present evidence is not a blanket benchmark win over scalar baselines, but a selector with explicit priority guarantees, infeasibility signaling, and auditable decision logic.

Verification supports the practical reliability of the stack: semantic invariants pass at 99.2--100\%, post-mitigation NaN/Inf propagation is 0.0\%, and proxy--full-evaluator pairwise candidate-ranking concordance reaches 0.948.  Mean inference latency is 7.3\,ms per sample, and a 55-scenario Waymax log-replay study spanning 3{,}338.5\,s records zero overlap steps, confirming end-to-end integration of the selection pipeline.  Taken together, the results suggest that explicit rule-prioritized selection is a practical way to make multi-modal prediction outputs more accountable, interpretable, and compliance-oriented without retraining the underlying generator.  Limitations, scope boundaries, and deployment qualifications are consolidated in Sections~\ref{Sec:Known_Gaps_and_Next_Steps_Toward_Deployment} and~\ref{Sec:Conclusion}.
\end{abstract}
